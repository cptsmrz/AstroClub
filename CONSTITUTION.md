## PREAMBLE

Before anything else, we are members of AstroClub. Not presidents, not heads, not secretaries — members first. That is where every one of us starts and where we remain, regardless of what responsibilities we carry.

AstroClub, GLA University, Mathura runs under the guidance of the Center for Cosmology, Astrophysics & Space Science (CCASS). It is a student-run club, built by students, for students. We look at stars, handle telescopes, run events, do research, and try to pass something worth inheriting to whoever comes next.

This constitution exists because informal rules are fine until they are not. It is written to protect the club from bad decisions, unclear authority, and the natural chaos of annual leadership change. It does not cover everything. Where it falls short, the Advisory Committee, the Director, and common sense fill the gap.

Every council that comes after us will find this document waiting. It is their starting point, not their ceiling.

## ARTICLE I: NAME AND PURPOSE

### Section 1: Official Name
The name of this organization shall be AstroClub, GLA University, Mathura, hereinafter referred to as "the Club."

### Section 2: Purpose and Objectives
* **(a) Primary Mission:** AstroClub is an observational astronomy organization. Hands-on telescope operation, practical learning, and individual research under CCASS faculty supervision are its core activities.
* **(b) Core Activities:**
  * Operation and maintenance of the Club's telescopes (Newtonian and Galilean) and astronomical equipment
  * Regular and bi-annual stargazing sessions for members and the university community
  * An annual campus-wide Astronomy Quiz competition
  * Support for individual student research projects under CCASS faculty supervision
* **(c) Educational Goals:**
  * Build practical observational and telescope operation skills
  * Create a community of astronomy enthusiasts and aspiring researchers within GLA University
  * Conduct outreach to promote astronomical literacy on campus and beyond
* **(d) Institutional Affiliation:** The Club operates under the guidance and oversight of CCASS, GLA University, and follows all university policies.

### Section 3: Non-Discrimination Policy
AstroClub shall not discriminate on the basis of caste, religion, race, gender, sexual orientation, disability, or socioeconomic status. Membership is open to all students of GLA University in accordance with the Constitution of India and university anti-discrimination policies.

### Section 4: Non-Profit Status
AstroClub is a non-profit student organization. It does not operate for commercial gain. All funds collected through membership fees, sponsorships, or event revenue go exclusively toward club activities, equipment maintenance, and member welfare. No profits are distributed to any individual member or officer.

## ARTICLE II: MEMBERSHIP

### Section 1: Categories of Participation
* **(e) General Members (Probationary):** Students approved for membership who are in their mandatory 2-week probationary period before fee payment.
* **(f) Members:** Students who have completed probation and paid the membership fee. Members who remain active for 6 consecutive weeks automatically become Core Members.
* **(g) Core Members:** Members with 6 weeks of sustained active participation. Eligible for selection to officer and team positions.
* **(h) Officers and Team Members:** Core Members selected to leadership or team roles as defined in Article III.
* **(i) Volunteers:** Non-members who may participate in club activities under direct supervision of a Member or higher. Restricted access to club resources.
* **(j) Advisory Committee:** Former officers who retain advisory capacity as defined in Article III, Section 10.

### Section 2: Membership Application and Approval
* **(k) Membership is open to all currently enrolled students of GLA University without discrimination as specified in Article I, Section 3.**
* **(l) Prospective members must obtain approval from the President, Vice President, and General Secretary (collectively, "the Approving Officers") to begin probation.**
* **(m) Upon approval, applicants enter a mandatory 2-week probationary period as General Members.**
* **(n) General Members may not access club equipment during probation.**

### Section 3: Financial Obligations
* **(o) Upon completing the 2-week probationary period, General Members must pay a one-time mandatory membership fee of Rs. 60\* to cover the production and issuance of their official AstroClub ID card, to attain full Member status.**
* **(p) Official AstroClub T-shirts are entirely optional and may be purchased separately by members at cost price based on prevailing vendor rates at the time of purchase.**
* **(q) Membership fees are non-refundable. ID card fees (Rs. 60\*) may be refunded only if the card has not yet been printed.**
* **(r) Members reinstating after a ban must pay Rs. 60\* for a replacement ID card.**

*\* Fee amounts are subject to adjustment based on prevailing market prices for materials and production. The Executive Council sets actual fees annually and communicates them before payment.*

### Section 4: Rights and Privileges by Tier
* **(s) General Members (Probationary):** May attend general meetings and public events. No access to telescopes, equipment, or restricted activities.
* **(t) Members:** Full equipment access subject to Article VI approval procedures. Eligibility for all club activities, events, and workshops.
* **(u) Core Members:** All Member privileges plus eligibility for officer and team selection, and participation in strategic planning.
* **(v) Volunteers:** May participate under direct supervision of a Member or officer. Telescope access only when a Member or officer is physically present for the entire duration. No independent equipment access.
* **(w) Faculty Members:** Faculty and staff of GLA University may join as Members with full access rights. They do not participate in leadership selection processes.

### Section 5: Attendance and Participation Requirements
* **(x) Definition:** "Attendance" means participation in any formal call for club work — meetings, event support, project assignments, content deadlines, or task requests issued by officers.
* **(y) Tracking:** Officers issuing calls for club work report non-responses to the General Secretary, who maintains the official attendance log.
* **(z) Consecutive Absence Policy:**
  * First absence: No action
  * Second absence: Official written warning issued by the General Secretary
  * Third consecutive absence: Immediate termination of membership
* **(aa) Exemptions:** Members may be excused only if they provide advance notice with valid justification (examinations, medical emergencies, family obligations). Retroactive excuses are not accepted.
* **(bb) Ban Duration:** Members terminated for attendance violations are banned for the remainder of the current semester and the entirety of the following semester.

### Section 6: Reinstatement After Attendance Ban
* **(cc) Individuals may reapply after their ban expires.**
* **(dd) Reinstatement requires fresh approval from the Approving Officers and payment of Rs. 60\* for a replacement ID card. No additional probationary period.**

### Section 7: Meeting Frequency
* **(ee) A minimum of one mandatory in-person meeting per semester, typically at the start of semester.**
* **(ff) Up to two additional meetings per semester (online or offline) at the Executive Council's discretion.**
* **(gg) Team-specific meetings do not count toward the semester limit.**

### Section 8: Academic Standing Requirements
* **(hh) All position holders — Core Officers and Specialized Officers — must maintain throughout their tenure:**
  * President: Minimum CGPA of 7.0 on a 10-point scale
  * All other position holders (Core Officers and Specialized Officers): Minimum CGPA of 6.5 on a 10-point scale
  * No more than two (2) active backlogs for all position holders
  * No backlog older than one (1) academic year for all position holders
* **(ii) Academic standing is verified during the bi-semester membership audit in Section 10.**
* **(jj) A position holder falling below required standing receives a 30-day notice to rectify. Failure results in removal from their position. General membership status is retained unless other grounds for termination exist.**
* **(kk) Academic standing requirements apply to position holders only.**

### Section 9: Code of Conduct
* **(ll) All members of AstroClub are expected to conduct themselves with professionalism, integrity, and mutual respect in their capacity as club members.**
* **(mm) The following constitute violations:**
  * Disrespect toward fellow members, officers, faculty, or external collaborators
  * Misuse or negligent handling of club equipment
  * Misrepresentation of the club to external parties without authorization
  * Badmouthing the club on personal or public platforms. Critique — positive or negative — is welcome through proper internal channels. Public badmouthing results in immediate removal.
  * Breach of confidentiality regarding club operations or internal matters
  * Any conduct unbecoming of a member of AstroClub
* **(nn) Violations are reported to the Vice President. If the complaint involves the Vice President, it escalates directly to the President.**
* **(oo) The accused receives a 24-hour conflict resolution window before any action is taken.**
* **(pp) Post window, disciplinary action is at the Executive Council's discretion.**

### Section 10: Membership Audit
* **(qq) A formal audit of all members and position holders shall be conducted twice per semester by the General Secretary in consultation with the Executive Council.**
* **(rr) The audit verifies active participation, academic standing of position holders, fee payment status, and equipment log compliance.**
* **(ss) Members or position holders found in violation are formally notified within 48 hours of the audit conclusion.**
* **(tt) A 24-hour conflict resolution window is provided to any member facing punitive action from the audit.**

### Section 11: Removal and Blacklisting
* **(uu) Removal Authority:** The President holds sole discretionary authority to remove a member or position holder following the conflict resolution window.
* **(vv) Written Justification:** Any removal must be documented in writing. A single clear bullet point stating the reason is sufficient. This record is filed permanently by the General Secretary.
* **(ww) Blacklisting:** A formally removed member is permanently blacklisted from:
  * Active membership in AstroClub
  * Participation in all club activities and events
  * Access to club telescopes, equipment, and future inventory — even under supervision
  * Any formal or informal professional association with the club
* **(xx) Council Obligation:** All Executive Council members and position holders must sever all professional ties with the blacklisted individual in their capacity as club representatives.
* **(yy) Blacklist Record:** The General Secretary maintains a permanent record passed to every incoming council.
* **(zz) Distinction:** Blacklisting is permanent and absolute, distinct from the attendance ban in Section 5 which is temporary.

### Section 12: Resignation and Reinstatement
* **(aaa) Resignation from a Position:** Any position holder wishing to resign their role must first have a direct conversation with the Vice President, who shall make reasonable efforts to resolve the concern. If unresolvable, the individual submits a formal resignation via email to astroclub@gla.ac.in, copied to the General Secretary. Resignation takes effect upon acknowledgment by the General Secretary.
* **(bbb) Resignation from the Club:** Resigning a position and resigning from the club are separate acts. A position holder who steps down retains full club membership and pays no additional fee unless they explicitly choose to leave the club entirely.
* **(ccc) Reinstatement After Resignation:** A resigned member may apply for reinstatement after 45 days from the date of resignation. Reinstatement requires unanimous agreement from all four Core Officers and majority agreement from Specialized Officers. Reinstated members re-enter at General Member status. Standard membership progression applies. Only fee is Rs. 60\* for ID card replacement.
* **(ddd) Resignation vs Removal:** A resigned member may seek reinstatement after 45 days. A removed and blacklisted member has no reinstatement path.

## ARTICLE III: LEADERSHIP STRUCTURE AND GOVERNANCE

### Section 1: Member-First Principle
Every person in AstroClub — from the member joining today to the president who led for years — holds the same fundamental identity: club member. Roles like President, VP, or General Secretary are responsibilities, not ranks. No officer is above a member in identity. This principle governs how the club operates and how its people carry themselves.

### Section 2: Executive Council Composition
* **(eee) Core Officers (Mandatory Annual Transition Positions):**
  * President
  * Vice President
  * General Secretary
  * Technical Head
* **(fff) Specialized Officers:**
  * Treasurer
  * Astrophotography Head
  * Telescope Handler
  * Design Head
  * Chief Editor
  * Content Head
  * Website Head
  * Media Head
* **(ggg) Standing Committees:** PR & Branding Committee (composed of President, Vice President, Astrophotography Head, Content Head, and designated members).
* **(hhh) Team Members:** Individuals selected to support Specialized Officers in their functional areas.

### Section 3: Term and Transition Schedule
* **(iii) Official term of office:** February 1 to January 31 of the following year.
* **(jjj) Core Officer Eligibility:** As of February 1, all Core Officers (President, Vice President, General Secretary, Technical Head) must be in their second year of study. No student in their first year may hold a Core Officer position, subject to the narrow exception in Section 4(a).
* **(kkk) Mandatory Core Officer Transition:** If the President, VP, GS, or Technical Head is entering their third year of study as of February 1, they must vacate the position.
* **(lll) Continuity for Specialized Officers:** Specialized Officers may continue across multiple terms regardless of year of study, subject to willingness and council approval.
* **(mmm) Transition Period:** February 1 through March 31. New President assumes authority on February 1. Former President provides active mentorship during this period. Full independence is effective April 1. The Former President and the president from two years prior remain available for guidance beyond April.

### Section 4: President
* **(nnn) Eligibility:** The President must be in their second year of study as of February 1. On an extremely rare basis, a first-year student may be considered for the presidency only if they satisfy all three of the following conditions simultaneously:
  1. *Academic Excellence:* Minimum CGPA of 7.5 on a 10-point scale.
  2. *Exceptional Astronomical Knowledge:* Demonstrated mastery of astronomy and astrophysics that leaves all peers and seniors clearly behind. This is assessed through the outgoing President's judgment in consultation with the VP, GS, Advisory Committee, and Director, CCASS.
  3. *Unconditional Commitment:* Demonstrable willingness to place the club above personal priorities in all but rare and genuine family obligations.
  *Failure to satisfy even one of the three conditions disqualifies a first-year candidate entirely. This exception is not a pathway — it is a last resort when no suitable second-year candidate exists.*
* **(ooo) Selection Criteria:** The outgoing President evaluates all candidates — first or second year — on integrity, character, leadership qualities, knowledge of astronomy and astrophysics, and long-term suitability for the club's mission. Public visibility during the semester is not a criterion — the worthy must bear the burden regardless of prior profile.
* **(ppp) Selection Authority:** The outgoing President has final authority to select their successor. The VP and GS actively critique and question the selection in the interest of the club. The Former President advises. Director, CCASS is informed of the decision.
* **(qqq) Duties and Responsibilities:**
  * Official representative of AstroClub to university administration and external organizations
  * Provides strategic vision and direction
  * Oversees all club operations and activities
  * Holds final decision-making authority on matters not delegated to other officers
  * Leads the selection process for VP, GS, and Technical Head
  * Co-leads the PR & Branding Committee
* **(rrr) Authority:**
  * Approves or denies membership applications with VP and GS
  * Serves as authorized approver for telescope equipment access
  * May delegate specific responsibilities to other officers
  * Holds sole discretionary authority to remove members and initiate blacklisting

* **(xxx) Duties:**

* **(oooo) Veto Authority Holders:** The Former President holds primary veto authority, extended to the Former Vice President and Former General Secretary.
* **(pppp) Role:** The Advisory Committee advises the current council, preserves the core ethos of AstroClub, and exercises veto authority when warranted.
* **(qqqq) Grounds for Veto:**
  1. *Character Concerns (Highest Priority):* Demonstrated lack of character, disrespectful conduct, or behaviour unbecoming of club leadership.

  1. Advisory Committee member identifies grounds for removal.

  4. Delivers letter to the affected officer and Director, CCASS.
* **(ssss) Effect:** Upon valid veto execution, the officer is immediately removed and vacancy procedures in Section 12 take effect.

* **(tttt) Director, CCASS (A. Pradhan):** Provides ultimate oversight and guidance. Highest authority on matters of governance and university compliance. Receives notification of all veto actions and significant decisions. May intervene when necessary to ensure alignment with CCASS and university objectives.
* **(uuuu) Faculty In-Charge:** Official signing authority for club documents requiring faculty approval. Provides day-to-day supervision and administrative support. Liaises between club leadership and university administration.

### Section 12: Vacancies
* **(vvvv) Presidential Vacancy:** Vice President assumes Acting President immediately. Advisory Committee and current GS select permanent replacement within 14 days.
* **(wwww) Core Officer Vacancies (VP, GS, Technical Head):** President in consultation with Advisory Committee and remaining core officers selects replacement within 14 days.
* **(xxxx) Specialized Officer Vacancies:** Filled through standard selection in Section 9(a). President may appoint interim officer until permanent selection is made.

### Section 13: Officer Resignation Protocol
* **(yyyy) Officers resigning their position follow Article II, Section 12. Vacancy procedures in Section 12 take immediate effect upon resignation.**
* **(zzzz) An officer may not abandon their post without completing the resignation procedure. Abandonment is grounds for removal and blacklisting at the President's discretion.**
* **(aaaaa) Outgoing officers must complete a formal handover including transfer of all relevant documents, digital access credentials, and briefing of the incoming officer.**

* **(bbbbb) General Club Meetings (Top Priority):** Formal assemblies of all members called by the Executive Council. These take precedence over all other club activities.

* **(ddddd) Team Meetings (High Priority):** Called by specialized officers for their respective teams only. No Executive Council approval required but must be reported to the General Secretary.

* **(eeeee) General Club Meetings:** Only Executive Officers (President, VP, GS, Technical Head).
* **(fffff) Executive Council Meetings:** Any core officer at their discretion.
* **(ggggg) Team Meetings:** The relevant specialized officer for their team only.
* **(hhhhh) Emergency Meetings:** Less than 2 days notice permitted with authorization from the President or VP, with mandatory agreement from the GS.

### Section 3: Notice Requirements
* **(iiiii) General Club Meetings require a minimum of two (2) days advance notice.**
* **(jjjjj) Notice is issued through official club communication channels.**

### Section 4: Meeting Frequency and Format
* **(lllll) Mandatory:** Minimum one in-person General Club Meeting at the start of each semester.
* **(mmmmm) Additional:** Up to two additional meetings per semester (online or offline). Total whole-club meetings shall not exceed three per semester.
* **(nnnnn) Executive Council Meetings:** At core officers' discretion based on operational needs. Specialized officers invited as needed.
* **(ooooo) Team Meetings:** At the relevant team head's discretion. Do not count toward the semester limit.

### Section 5: Agenda
* **(ppppp) A formal written agenda is prepared for approximately 60% of scheduled meetings where sufficient preparation time exists.**

* **(sssss) Emergency meetings are exempt from formal agenda requirements.**

### Section 6: Conduct of Meetings
* **(ttttt) General Club Meetings:** Presided over by the President, or in their absence, the Vice President.

### Section 7: Minutes and Record Keeping
* **(wwwww) The General Secretary, or a designated team member, records official minutes for all General Club and Executive Council meetings.**
* **(xxxxx) Team meeting notes are maintained by the relevant team head and submitted to the General Secretary.**
* **(yyyyy) All minutes are permanently retained in the club's official records archive.**
* **(zzzzz) Minutes are accessible to all active club members upon request.**

### Section 8: Attendance at Meetings
* **(aaaaaa) Attendance requirements in Article II, Section 5 apply to all calls for club work including meetings.**
* **(bbbbbb) Members must provide advance notice of inability to attend. Retroactive excuses are not accepted.**
* **(cccccc) Executive Council Meeting attendance by general members is at the presiding officer's sole discretion.**

  * If multiple sponsors: exact financial split between each party
  * If university funding is included: specific items and amounts covered
  * Miscellaneous expenses and their source of coverage
* **(llllll) The President holds final approval authority over all expenditures. The Treasurer executes approved payments.**

### Section 4: Equipment and Asset Funding
* **(mmmmmm) Telescopes and major astronomical equipment fall under CCASS property and financial responsibility. Equipment acquisition requests are made by the Director, CCASS directly to the Vice Chancellor.**

### Section 5: Sponsorship Management
* **(pppppp) Any member of the Outreach Team, Treasurer, or Executive Council may pursue and secure sponsorships on behalf of the club.**
* **(qqqqqq) The individual who secures a sponsor becomes the designated point of contact for that sponsor for the duration of the agreement.**
* **(rrrrrr) All sponsorship agreements require Executive Council review and approval before finalization.**
* **(ssssss) Sponsorship terms, amounts, and obligations are documented and filed with the General Secretary.**

* **(tttttt) University Credit Line Surplus:** Unspent university-sanctioned funds remain at GLA University's discretion and do not roll over.
* **(uuuuuu) Sponsorship and Event Revenue Surplus:** Unspent funds roll over to the next council and are accounted for in the incoming Treasurer's opening balance.

### Section 7: Financial Irregularities and Escalation
* **(vvvvvv) The Treasurer may flag financial irregularities of any size.**
* **(wwwwww) Escalation ladder:**
  1. Raise with VP and GS first and attempt internal resolution within 5 working days.
  2. If unresolved, report formally to the Faculty In-Charge.
* **(xxxxxx) The Treasurer's right to flag irregularities cannot be overridden by the President.**

### Section 8: Financial Review and Audit
* **(yyyyyy) Financial reviews are conducted during Executive Council meetings with the Treasurer presenting:**
  * Income received by source
  * Expenditures made with approval records
  * Current available balance by funding category
  * Pending financial obligations
* **(zzzzzz) The Treasurer maintains permanent financial records archived with the General Secretary.**
* **(aaaaaaa) All financial records are subject to review by the Director, CCASS upon request.**

## ARTICLE VI: EQUIPMENT MANAGEMENT

### Section 1: Scope
* **(bbbbbbb) This Article governs use, access, and accountability procedures for all astronomical equipment owned by or entrusted to AstroClub.**
* **(ccccccc) All telescopes and major astronomical equipment remain the property of CCASS, GLA University. The club is responsible for their responsible use and maintenance.**

### Section 2: Equipment Inventory
* **(ddddddd) The club currently operates:**
  * One (1) Newtonian Reflector Telescope
  * One (1) Galilean Telescope
* **(eeeeeee) The Telescope Handler maintains a current inventory of all equipment and their condition, updated after every use and audited monthly.**

### Section 3: Access Eligibility
* **(fffffff) Independent Access (with approval):** Members and Core Members.
* **(ggggggg) Supervised Access Only:** Volunteers — only when a Member or officer is physically present for the entire duration.
* **(hhhhhhh) No Access:** General Members (probationary) and blacklisted individuals under any circumstances, including supervised access.

### Section 5: Authorized Approvers
* **(lllllll) The following may approve equipment access requests:**
  * President
  * Vice President
  * General Secretary
  * Astrophotography Head
  * Former President (Advisory Committee)
  * Director, CCASS (A. Pradhan)
* **(mmmmmmm) When an approver is also the requestor:** No secondary approval required. The approver-requestor sends an email to astroclub@gla.ac.in with the President copied.
* **(nnnnnnn) When the President is the requestor:** Email to astroclub@gla.ac.in with Director, CCASS copied.
* **(ooooooo) CCASS Faculty:** No approval required. Standard log-in and log-out procedures remain mandatory.
* **(ppppppp) Non-CCASS Faculty:** Access only under direct supervision of a club Executive Council member or CCASS faculty member for the entire duration.

### Section 6: Equipment Log
* **(qqqqqqq) A physical Equipment Log (Telescope Diary) is maintained in the office of the Director, CCASS.**
* **(rrrrrrr) Log-In Entry (before use):**
  * Date and time of checkout
  * Name of member taking equipment
  * Equipment taken
  * Purpose and duration
  * Name of approver
  * Approver's signature
* **(sssssss) Log-Out Entry (upon return):**
  * Date and time of return
  * Condition of equipment upon return
  * Member's signature
* **(ttttttt) Approvers taking equipment themselves complete both the taker and approver fields with their own name.**
* **(uuuuuuu) The Equipment Log is audited daily by the Director, CCASS or designated staff.**
* **(vvvvvvv) Equipment is under constant camera surveillance. All interactions with equipment are recorded.**

### Section 7: Damage and Liability
* **(wwwwwww) Normal wear and tear is expected and accepted.**
* **(xxxxxxx) Negligent Damage:** Both the member who used the equipment and the approver who authorized access bear shared responsibility. Final determination rests with the Director, CCASS. The university may issue a formal fine notice. Outstanding fines must be cleared before the responsible member may appear in university examinations.
* **(yyyyyyy) This constitution does not override university disciplinary procedures in matters of equipment damage.**

### Section 8: Equipment Handling Training
* **(zzzzzzz) All new members receive mandatory telescope handling and safety training during their first six weeks of active membership.**
* **(aaaaaaaa) Training is conducted by the Telescope Handler in coordination with the Astrophotography Head.**
* **(bbbbbbbb) Training covers:**
  * Safe assembly and disassembly of all club telescopes
  * Proper handling, transport, and storage procedures
  * Basic maintenance and troubleshooting
  * Correct observation techniques for Newtonian and Galilean telescopes
  * Emergency procedures in case of equipment malfunction or accidental damage
* **(cccccccc) Completion of training does not replace the approval procedure in Section 4.**

### Section 9: Weather and Force Majeure

## ARTICLE X: ANTI-HAZING POLICY

### Section 1: Statement of Commitment
AstroClub is committed to maintaining a safe, inclusive, and respectful environment for all members. The club does not condone hazing in any form.

### Section 2: Definition of Hazing
Hazing is any intentional act committed individually or collectively against a prospective, current, or former member that causes or creates risk of physical or psychological harm, involves personal degradation or humiliation, or is conducted as a condition of membership, initiation, or affiliation with the club.

### Section 3: Prohibition
* **(sssssssss) Hazing is strictly prohibited within AstroClub and at all club-related activities, events, and functions.**
* **(ttttttttt) Consent of the subjected individual is not a defense or justification.**
* **(uuuuuuuuu) Hazing violations are reported immediately to the Vice President and Director, CCASS.**

### Section 4: Consequences
Any member found to have participated in or facilitated hazing faces immediate removal and permanent blacklisting from AstroClub, in addition to referral to GLA University's disciplinary authorities.
